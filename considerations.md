# Practical Considerations

When evaluating listings, please consider these practical factors:

## Financial Transparency
- **Additional monthly costs** (servicekosten): What are they? Are they reasonable?
- **Utilities included or separate**: Gas, water, electricity (g/w/e)
- **Taxes and fees**: Municipal taxes, waste collection
- **Deposit requirements**: How much upfront?
- **Hidden costs**: Maintenance fees, parking fees

## Landlord/Listing Reputation
- **Landlord/agency reputation**: Are they known and reliable?
- **Listing authenticity**: Does the listing seem legitimate or potentially a scam?
- **Reviews or history**: Any red flags or positive signals about the property management?
- **Response time and professionalism**: How responsive are they?

## Utilities & Internet
- **Gas/Water/Electricity (g/w/e)**: Included or separate? Average costs?
- **Heating type**: Central heating, individual boiler, district heating?
- **WiFi/Internet availability**: Fiber available? Speed options? Provider restrictions?
- **Energy label**: Better labels = lower utility costs

## Neighborhood Amenities
- **Grocery stores nearby**: Albert Heijn, Jumbo, Lidl within walking distance?
- **Public transport**: Bus/train stations, bike paths
- **Parks and green spaces**: Nearby for walking, exercise, relaxation
- **Safety**: Well-lit streets, safe bike parking, low crime area
- **Noise levels**: Near bars/nightlife or quiet residential?

## Long-term Livability
- **Contract length**: Temporary or indefinite? Minimum rental period?
- **Maintenance responsibility**: Who handles repairs?
- **Building condition**: Recently renovated or outdated?
- **Storage space**: Basement, attic, bike storage
- **Parking**: Street parking available? Private spot? Cost?

## Key Questions to Ask
When a listing looks promising:
- What are the total monthly costs including utilities and servicekosten?
- Is the landlord/agency reputable and responsive?
- What grocery stores and parks are within 10-minute walk?
- Is fiber internet available?
- What's the energy label and typical monthly g/w/e costs?
